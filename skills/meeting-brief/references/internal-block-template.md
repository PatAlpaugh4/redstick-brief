# Internal Meeting Block — Template

Use this exact format for every meeting where ALL attendees are `@redstickvc.com`.

```
<TIME> · <MEETING_DESCRIPTOR>

LAST TIME
<2–4 lines on what was agreed last time you talked. What he owes you, what you owe him. Cite emails by date.>

OPEN BETWEEN YOU
- <Specific bullet: thing pending, e.g., "Marcus's references on Acme — he sent Thursday, you owe a read">
- <Another: "Q3 LP letter draft — you owe him the Greenhouse section">
- <Another: "His Friday flight — confirm you're covering the Carter call">

ONE THING
<Single most important topic to confirm or surface. The thing that, if you forgot to bring up, would cost you. Often it's something the other person doesn't know yet.>
```

## Section rules

- **<MEETING_DESCRIPTOR>** examples: `1:1 with Marcus`, `Partner sync`, `Portfolio review`, `Fund I review`, `Operations standup`. Pick what reads naturally given the event title.
- **LAST TIME** is the recap. Be specific: "You agreed he'd own Acme reference checks; you'd push back on Greenhouse's valuation." Don't generalize as "discussed open items."
- **OPEN BETWEEN YOU** is a bulleted list of specific commitments — sent but not read, sections owed, schedule conflicts, decisions pending. **No generic items.** "Discuss strategy" is not a bullet.
- **ONE THING** is the single most important item to surface in the meeting. Often it's a piece of news the other person doesn't know yet (e.g., "Carter Family Office emailed yesterday wanting an earlier call. Marcus doesn't know yet."). If there's no genuinely important "one thing," cut the section.

## Citation format

Inline, italic, parenthetical — same as external blocks:

- `*(per Thursday email)*`
- `*(his calendar invite this morning)*`
- `*(Slack DM yesterday)*`

## What to cut

- No FIRST MOVE for internal blocks (that's external-only).
- No WHAT'S NEW for internal blocks (no public-research pass for partners).
- No diligence jargon (MOIC, TVPI, EV) — same rule as external.
- If LAST TIME and OPEN BETWEEN YOU are both thin, collapse to a one-line block: `<TIME> · <DESCRIPTOR> — no recent threads since <date>; usual cadence.`

## Length target

5–12 lines total per block. Internal blocks are lighter than external by design.
